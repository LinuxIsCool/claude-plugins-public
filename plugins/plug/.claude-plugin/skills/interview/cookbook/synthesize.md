# Interview Synthesis Cookbook

Consolidate interview findings into actionable intelligence.

## Synthesis Process

### 1. Gather Raw Insights

Review all responses and extract:
- Direct statements of intent
- Implicit preferences
- Stated constraints
- Emotional signals (enthusiasm, concern, uncertainty)
- Contradictions or tensions

### 2. Theme Clustering

Group insights into categories:

| Category | Contents |
|----------|----------|
| **Goals** | Desired outcomes, success criteria |
| **Constraints** | Technical, resource, time, organizational limits |
| **Priorities** | Ranked importance, trade-off preferences |
| **Risks** | Concerns, failure modes, dependencies |
| **Stakeholders** | Users, decision-makers, affected parties |
| **Open Questions** | Unresolved items needing further exploration |

### 3. Pattern Recognition

Identify:
- **Convergent themes**: Ideas mentioned multiple times
- **Divergent signals**: Contradictory requirements
- **Gaps**: Areas not covered that may need attention
- **Surprises**: Unexpected insights or constraints

### 4. Priority Mapping

Create a simple priority matrix:

```
                 High Impact
                     │
                     │
     Quick Wins      │     Strategic
                     │
    ─────────────────┼─────────────────
                     │
     Low Priority    │     Foundations
                     │
                     │
                 Low Impact
    ← Easy                    Hard →
```

Place key initiatives/requirements in quadrants.

## Validation Check

Before finalizing synthesis, verify:

- [ ] All major interview themes captured
- [ ] No contradictions left unaddressed
- [ ] Priorities are ranked, not just listed
- [ ] Next steps are specific and actionable
- [ ] Open questions identified for follow-up

## Handoff

After synthesis, offer the user:

1. **Save findings** to a file (optional)
2. **Proceed to action** on recommended next steps
3. **Deeper dive** on any specific area
4. **Additional interview** for unexplored domains
