# Non-Profits

Non-profit structures in Canada, with primary focus on BC Societies Act and CRA charitable registration.

## Entity Overview

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Primary Use** | Activities not for private gain; public benefit |
| **Liability** | Limited (society is separate legal entity) |
| **Taxation** | Generally exempt; charities can issue tax receipts |
| **Governance** | Members elect directors; no shareholders |
| **Duration** | Perpetual existence |

## Key Distinction: Non-Profit vs Charity

### Non-Profit (Society)

- Must not distribute profits to members
- Purpose can be almost any lawful activity
- Does NOT automatically mean tax-exempt
- Cannot issue donation tax receipts
- Less CRA oversight

### Registered Charity

- Must be registered with CRA
- Must have exclusively charitable purposes
- CAN issue donation tax receipts
- Tax-exempt on most income
- Significant CRA reporting requirements
- Restrictions on business activities

## [BC] BC Societies Act

### Eligible Purposes

Under the Societies Act, purposes can include:
- Agricultural or horticultural
- Artistic or cultural
- Athletic or recreational
- Charitable or benevolent
- Educational or literary
- Environmental or conservation
- Political (advocacy)
- Professional or trade
- Social or community benefit
- Any other lawful purpose

### Incorporation Process

1. **Name Approval**: Reserve name with BC Registry
2. **Constitution**: Set out purposes
3. **Bylaws**: Governance rules
4. **Filing**: Submit to BC Registry
5. **Directors**: Minimum 3 (vs 1 for corporations)

### Governance Structure

**Members**:
- Own the society (in governance sense)
- Vote on major decisions
- Elect directors
- Approve bylaw changes
- No economic interest (cannot receive profits)

**Directors**:
- Manage the society
- Fiduciary duties similar to corporate directors
- Minimum 3 directors
- No residency requirement

**Officers**:
- Appointed by directors
- President, secretary, treasurer, etc.
- Handle day-to-day operations

### Member Classes

Can have multiple member classes:
- Voting members
- Non-voting members
- Honorary members
- Different rights and obligations

### Distribution Prohibition

**Fundamental Rule**: Society must not distribute any part of income or property to members.

Exceptions:
- Reasonable remuneration for services
- Benefits to members that further society purposes
- Distribution on dissolution to similar organization

### Dissolution

On dissolution, remaining assets must go to:
- Another society with similar purposes
- A registered charity
- The Crown

Cannot be distributed to members.

## CRA Charitable Registration

### Charitable Purposes

CRA recognizes four heads of charity:
1. **Relief of poverty**
2. **Advancement of education**
3. **Advancement of religion**
4. **Other purposes beneficial to community**

The fourth category requires demonstrating public benefit.

### Registration Process

1. **Confirm eligibility**: Purposes must be charitable
2. **Prepare application**: T2050 form
3. **Gather documents**: Constitution, bylaws, financial projections
4. **Submit to CRA**: Can take 6-12 months
5. **CRA review**: May request changes to purposes
6. **Receive registration**: BN with RR extension

### Ongoing Compliance

**Annual Return (T3010)**:
- Due within 6 months of fiscal year-end
- Financial information
- Program information
- Director/officer information
- Compensation disclosure

**Disbursement Quota**:
- Must spend 3.5% of investment assets on charitable activities annually
- Plus any amounts received as gifts (10-year rule)

**Receipting Rules**:
- Can only receipt for gifts (not services, goods at FMV)
- Specific receipt format required
- Must maintain records

### Business Activities

Registered charities can conduct business activities IF:
- Related to charitable purposes, OR
- Substantially run by volunteers

**Excessive business activity** can result in:
- Revocation of charitable status
- Penalties
- Requirement to spin off to separate corporation

### Private Benefit Restrictions

Charities must not provide private benefit except incidentally.

**Prohibited**:
- Excessive compensation to related parties
- Preferential treatment to founders/donors
- Personal use of charitable property

### Political Activities

**Current Rules** (post-2018 amendments):
- Charities CAN engage in political activities
- Cannot support or oppose political parties
- Cannot be primarily political in purpose

## Comparison: Society vs CCPC

| Factor | BC Society | CCPC |
|--------|------------|------|
| Profit distribution | Prohibited | Allowed |
| Tax receipts | Only if registered charity | No |
| Governance | Member-based | Shareholder-based |
| Directors | Minimum 3 | Minimum 1 |
| Dissolution | Assets to similar org | Assets to shareholders |
| Fundraising | Donations, grants, fees | Investment, loans, revenue |
| Best for | Community benefit | Private gain |

## Hybrid Structures

### Non-Profit with Subsidiary Corporation

- Society holds shares of for-profit corporation
- Corporation conducts revenue-generating activities
- Profits flow to society for mission
- Maintains charitable/non-profit status

### Social Enterprise Models

- B Corps (not a legal structure in Canada, certification only)
- Community Contribution Company (BC) - limited distribution to shareholders
- Cooperative structures

## Common Scenarios

### Community Organization

**Recommendation**: BC Society (not charity)
- Simpler compliance
- More flexible purposes
- No CRA registration unless receipting needed
- Suitable for clubs, associations, community groups

### Charitable Foundation

**Recommendation**: BC Society + CRA registration
- Enables tax-receipted donations
- Grants to other charities or own programs
- More complex compliance
- Suitable for philanthropic activities

### Arts Organization

**Recommendation**: Depends on funding model
- If donor-funded: Register as charity
- If fee-based (performances): Society may suffice
- Consider hybrid with subsidiary for commercial activities

### Advocacy Group

**Recommendation**: BC Society (non-charity)
- Political activities more flexible
- No disbursement quota
- Cannot issue tax receipts
- Suitable for lobbying, policy work

## Compliance Checklist

### BC Society Annual Requirements

- [ ] Annual General Meeting (AGM)
- [ ] File annual report with BC Registry
- [ ] Maintain member and director registers
- [ ] Keep minutes of meetings
- [ ] Financial statements

### Registered Charity Annual Requirements

- [ ] All society requirements above
- [ ] T3010 annual return to CRA
- [ ] Track disbursement quota
- [ ] Maintain donation receipts and records
- [ ] Public disclosure of certain information

## Tax Considerations

### Non-Profit Society (Not Charity)

- Generally not taxable on member-funded activities
- MAY be taxable on property income, business income
- File T2 if any taxable income

### Registered Charity

- Exempt from income tax
- Exempt from GST on most activities
- May be exempt from property tax (varies by municipality)
- Still file T3010 annual return

### Donation Tax Credits

For donors to registered charities:
- First $200: 15% federal + provincial credits
- Above $200: 29% federal + provincial credits (more for top earners)
- Can carry forward 5 years
- Cannot exceed 75% of net income (exceptions apply)
