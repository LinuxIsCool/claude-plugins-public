# Securities Law

Canadian securities law fundamentals for private companies, with focus on BC and national instruments.

## Securities Law Framework

### The Default Rule

**Prospectus Requirement**: Cannot trade (sell) securities without a prospectus filed with and receipted by securities regulators.

**Exception**: Prospectus exemptions allow sales without full prospectus.

### Regulators

**Provincial/Territorial**: Securities regulation is provincial jurisdiction
- BC: BC Securities Commission (BCSC)
- Ontario: Ontario Securities Commission (OSC)
- Alberta: Alberta Securities Commission (ASC)
- etc.

**National Coordination**: Canadian Securities Administrators (CSA)
- Harmonized rules via National Instruments (NI)
- Passport system for multi-province offerings

### Key Legislation

**BC Securities Act**: Provincial legislation
**National Instruments**: Harmonized CSA rules
- NI 45-106: Prospectus Exemptions
- NI 51-102: Continuous Disclosure
- NI 31-103: Registration Requirements
- NI 45-102: Resale of Securities

## Prospectus Exemptions

### Accredited Investor Exemption (s. 2.3)

**Most Common for VC/Angel Financing**

**Accredited Investor Definition** (includes):

**Individuals**:
- Income > $200K (individual) or $300K (with spouse) in each of last 2 years, expected this year
- Net financial assets > $1M (excluding home)
- Net assets > $5M
- Person registered as adviser or dealer

**Entities**:
- Corporation with net assets > $5M
- Investment fund
- Pension fund
- Government
- Bank, trust company, credit union
- Insurance company

**Process**:
1. Investor signs accredited investor certificate
2. Company verifies investor meets criteria
3. No maximum investment limit
4. File report of exempt distribution

### Family, Friends and Business Associates (s. 2.5)

**For Close Relations Only**

**Family Members**:
- Spouse, parent, grandparent, sibling, child
- Spouse's family members

**Close Personal Friends**:
- Known long enough to assess capability and trustworthiness
- NOT just social acquaintances

**Business Associates**:
- Director, officer, or control person of issuer
- Director/officer of affiliate
- Employee involved in business operations
- Investors under other exemptions
- Professional advisors

**Limits**: None specified, but relationship must be genuine
**Process**: Signed risk acknowledgment required

### Offering Memorandum Exemption (s. 2.9)

**Allows Broader Investor Base**

**Requirements**:
- Provide offering memorandum (disclosure document)
- Prescribed format (Form 45-106F2)
- Signed risk acknowledgment
- Right of rescission

**Investment Limits** (non-accredited investors):
- $10,000 in single issuer in 12 months
- $30,000 total if received suitability advice
- No limit for accredited investors

**Best For**: Wider investor base, crowdfunding-style raises

### Minimum Amount Investment (s. 2.10)

**For Large Investments**

**Requirements**:
- Minimum $150,000 investment
- Paid in cash at closing
- Not borrowed from issuer or related party

**Best For**: Sophisticated investors making large investments

### Private Issuer Exemption (s. 2.4)

**For True Private Companies**

**Private Issuer Requirements**:
- Not a reporting issuer
- Securities carry transfer restrictions (in constating documents)
- 50 or fewer beneficial holders (excluding employees)
- No distribution to public

**Permitted Purchasers**:
- Directors, officers, employees
- Existing security holders
- Family members
- Close personal friends
- Close business associates
- Accredited investors
- Certain others specified in rule

## Report of Exempt Distribution

### Filing Requirement

**Form 45-106F1**: Required for most exemptions
**Due**: Within 10 days of distribution
**File In**: Each province where purchasers reside

**Contents**:
- Issuer information
- Distribution details
- Purchaser information
- Exemption relied upon
- Certification

### Exemptions from Filing

- Private issuer exemption distributions
- Some family, friend, business associate
- Certain employee distributions

## Resale Restrictions

### The Hold Period

**General Rule**: Securities acquired under exemption cannot be freely traded.

**4-Month Hold** (from distribution date):
- Applies to securities of non-reporting issuers
- Can resell under exemptions during hold period
- After hold period, additional conditions may apply

### Legends

**Requirement**: Certificates must be legended indicating resale restrictions.

**Legend Example**: "Unless permitted under securities legislation, the holder of this security must not trade the security before [DATE - 4 months from distribution]."

### Resale Exemptions

**To Other Exempt Purchasers**: Can resell to accredited investors, etc.
**First Trade After Hold Period**: Additional conditions apply for non-reporting issuers

## Crowdfunding

### Startup Crowdfunding (BC/Other Jurisdictions)

**Limits**:
- Issuer: $500,000 per year
- Investor: $1,500 per issuer per year

**Requirements**:
- Use registered funding portal
- Prescribed disclosure document
- Risk warnings

### Equity Crowdfunding via OM

**Through Offering Memorandum**:
- Can use online platforms
- Investment limits apply
- Full OM required

## Common Structures

### SAFE Note

**Securities Analysis**:
- IS a security (investment contract)
- Requires exemption for distribution
- Same restrictions as equity

**Exemptions Used**:
- Accredited investor
- Family, friends, business associates
- Offering memorandum

### Convertible Note

**Securities Analysis**:
- Debt instrument = security
- Convertible feature = also security
- Requires exemption

### Stock Options

**Employee Exemptions**:
- Employee, director, officer grants generally exempt
- Conditions apply
- Limited if issued to consultants

## Private Placement Process

### Steps

1. **Determine Exemptions**: Which exemptions will you rely on?

2. **Prepare Documents**:
   - Subscription agreement
   - Shareholder agreement (if new investors)
   - Offering memorandum (if OM exemption)
   - Risk acknowledgment forms
   - Accredited investor certificates

3. **Investor Qualification**:
   - Verify exemption qualification
   - Collect signed certificates/acknowledgments

4. **Closing**:
   - Execute subscription agreements
   - Receive funds
   - Issue shares/certificates (with legends)
   - Update corporate records

5. **Filing**:
   - Report of exempt distribution (10 days)
   - Any other required filings

### Common Documents

**Subscription Agreement**:
- Commitment to purchase
- Price and number of securities
- Representations and warranties
- Exemption certification
- Risk acknowledgments

**Risk Acknowledgment** (for some exemptions):
- Prescribed form
- Must be signed before or at time of investment
- Acknowledges limited liquidity, risk of loss

## Offering Memorandum Contents

### Prescribed Form (45-106F2)

**Required Sections**:
- Summary
- Use of proceeds
- Business of issuer
- Directors and officers
- Capital structure
- Risk factors
- Financial statements
- Purchaser rights

**Important**: Material must be accurate; liability for misrepresentation.

## Penalties and Liability

### Regulatory Consequences

- Cease trade orders
- Fines
- Administrative penalties
- Disgorgement of profits

### Civil Liability

**For Misrepresentation**:
- Rescission right (for purchasers)
- Damages
- Due diligence defense

**Directors/Officers**:
- Personal liability possible
- Due diligence defense available

### Criminal

- Fraud
- Market manipulation
- False statements

## Common Scenarios

### Angel Round

**Typical Structure**:
- SAFE or convertible notes
- 5-10 investors
- $200K-$500K raised

**Exemptions**:
- Accredited investor (most)
- Family, friends, business associates (some)

### Series A

**Typical Structure**:
- Preferred shares
- 1-3 institutional investors
- $2M-$10M raised

**Exemptions**:
- Accredited investor
- Minimum amount ($150K+)

### Crowdfunding

**Typical Structure**:
- Common shares or SAFEs
- Many small investors
- $100K-$500K raised

**Exemptions**:
- Offering memorandum
- Startup crowdfunding (if available)

## Disclaimer

Securities law is complex and penalties for non-compliance are severe. This sub-skill provides general guidance only. Always consult qualified securities lawyers before issuing securities.
