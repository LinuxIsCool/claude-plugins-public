# British Columbia Specific

BC-specific business law, taxation, and ecosystem considerations.

## Corporate Law

### BC Business Corporations Act (BCBCA)

**Key Features**:
- Modern, flexible corporate statute
- Annual report filing (no AGM required for private)
- Unlimited liability companies (ULCs) available
- No residency requirements for directors
- Articles (not bylaws) govern internal affairs

### Incorporation

**Required Elements**:
- Name approval or numbered company
- Articles of incorporation
- Registered office in BC
- At least one director
- At least one share issued

**Filing Location**: BC Corporate Registry

**Costs**:
- Name approval: $31.58
- Incorporation: $351.54
- Annual report: $44.32

### Company Types

**BC Ltd/Inc**: Standard corporation
**BC ULC**: Unlimited liability (tax planning)
**BC Extraprovincial**: Out-of-province company registered in BC

### Annual Requirements

**Annual Report**: Due 2 months after anniversary date
- Can be filed online
- Updates registered office, directors, addresses
- No financial statements filed publicly

**Records Office**: Must maintain in BC
- Can be at registered office
- Or at records office specified in articles

### Directors

**Minimum**: One director
**Residency**: No Canadian residency requirement
**Qualifications**: 18+, not bankrupt, not found incapable

**Duties** (statutory):
- Act honestly and in good faith
- Exercise care, diligence, skill
- Comply with Act and articles

**Liability Protections**:
- Business judgment rule
- Reliance on experts
- Due diligence defense (statutory)

### Shareholders

**Rights** (default, can be modified):
- Vote (one vote per share default)
- Receive dividends if declared
- Receive property on dissolution
- Dissent and appraisal rights

**Shareholder Agreements**: Common for private companies
- Unanimous if restricts board powers
- Pre-emptive rights
- Drag-along/tag-along
- Shotgun/buyout provisions

### Share Structure

**Flexibility**: BCBCA allows creative structures

**Common Structures**:
- Common shares (voting, equity)
- Preferred shares (priority, preferences)
- Multiple classes (voting/non-voting)
- Series within classes

**Default**: If articles silent, one class with equal rights

## Provincial Taxation

### Corporate Tax (BC)

**Provincial Rates (2024-2025)**:

| Income Type | Rate |
|-------------|------|
| Small business (CCPC, first $500K) | 2% |
| General rate | 12% |

**Combined Federal + BC**:
- Small business income: 11% (9% federal + 2% BC)
- General income: 27% (15% federal + 12% BC)

**BC Small Business Rate**: Among lowest in Canada

### Personal Tax (BC)

**BC Tax Brackets (2024)**:

| Taxable Income | BC Rate |
|----------------|---------|
| $0 - $47,937 | 5.06% |
| $47,937 - $95,875 | 7.70% |
| $95,875 - $110,076 | 10.50% |
| $110,076 - $133,664 | 12.29% |
| $133,664 - $181,232 | 14.70% |
| $181,232 - $252,752 | 16.80% |
| Over $252,752 | 20.50% |

**Top Combined Rate**: ~53.5% (federal + BC)

### Provincial Credits

**BC SR&ED Credit**: 10% refundable for CCPCs (in addition to federal)

**Interactive Digital Media Tax Credit (IDMTC)**:
- 17.5% of eligible BC labour
- For interactive digital media products
- Developed primarily for sale/license

**Film and TV Credits**: Various credits for productions

### Property Transfer Tax

**Rates**:
- First $200K: 1%
- $200K - $2M: 2%
- Over $2M: 3%
- Additional 2% for foreign buyers (residential)

**Exemptions**:
- First-time home buyers (up to $500K)
- Family transfers
- Some corporate reorganizations

### Employer Health Tax

**Payroll Tax** (replaced MSP premiums):

| BC Payroll | Rate |
|------------|------|
| Under $500K | Exempt |
| $500K - $1.5M | 2.925% on amount over $500K |
| Over $1.5M | 1.95% on total |

**Note**: Small business threshold provides meaningful savings

## Regulatory Environment

### Business Registration

**BC Corporate Registry**:
- Incorporations
- Annual reports
- Changes (directors, address, name)
- Dissolutions

**OneStop Business Registry**:
- Provincial licenses
- WorkSafeBC
- PST registration

### Provincial Licenses

**PST Registration**:
- Required if selling taxable goods/services in BC
- 7% PST (not harmonized with GST)
- Some exemptions (software, many services)

**Business Licenses**:
- Municipal business licenses (where operating)
- Specific industry licenses (food, liquor, etc.)

### Employment

**Employment Standards Branch**:
- Complaints and enforcement
- ESA compliance

**WorkSafeBC**:
- Workplace safety
- Workers' compensation insurance
- Mandatory for most employers

**BC Human Rights Tribunal**:
- Discrimination complaints
- Employment-related human rights

## Real Estate

### Commercial Leases

**Not Covered by Residential Tenancy Act**: Commercial leases are pure contract

**Key Terms**:
- Base rent
- Additional rent (CAM, taxes, insurance)
- Lease type (gross, modified gross, triple net)
- Term and renewal options
- Tenant improvements
- Personal guarantee (common for startups)

### Speculation and Vacancy Tax

**Applicable Areas**: Vancouver, Victoria, other high-demand areas

**Rate**: Up to 2% annually on empty homes

**Corporate Exemptions**: Limited; primarily residential tax

## Industry-Specific

### Cannabis

**Provincial Regulator**: BC Liquor and Cannabis Regulation Branch

**Licenses**:
- Retail: Provincial license
- Production/Processing: Federal license + provincial

### Liquor

**BC Liquor and Cannabis Regulation Branch**:
- Manufacturer licenses (winery, brewery, distillery)
- Retail licenses
- Food primary/liquor primary

### Professional Services

**Regulated Professions**: Must register with professional body
- Lawyers (Law Society of BC)
- Accountants (CPABC)
- Engineers (EGBC)
- Architects (AIBC)

## Planning Considerations

### Location Decisions

**Vancouver**: Tech hub, talent, higher costs
**Victoria**: Government, tourism, lower costs
**Kelowna**: Growing tech scene, quality of life
**Regional**: Much lower costs, remote work enabling

### Structure Optimization

**BC Advantages**:
- Low small business rate (11% combined)
- IDMTC for digital media
- SR&ED provincial credit
- No director residency requirements

**Considerations**:
- PST (vs HST provinces) adds complexity
- Employer Health Tax above $500K payroll
- High personal tax rates at top brackets
